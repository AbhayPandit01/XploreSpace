Black Hole

Black holes have such strong gravity that nothing—not even light—can escape once it gets too close. That is why they look completely black!

The lightest known black hole is only 3.8 times the mass of the sun, while the most massive black hole observed is 66 billion times the mass of the sun.

All black holes spin, and the fastest known spins over 1,000 times per second.

Black holes can be small or huge. Some are just a few times the mass of the Sun, while others, called supermassive black holes, can be millions or even billions of times bigger than the Sun. Every large galaxy, including ours, has one at its center!

Parallel Universe

If parallel universes exist, there could be infinite versions of you living in different realities. In one universe, you might be a famous actor, while in another, you could be living in a completely different time period!

Some theories suggest that the strange feeling of déjà vu might be a momentary glimpse into a parallel universe where you've already experienced that event. It's a wild idea that adds a mystical twist to an ordinary phenomenon.

Ever had a dream that felt incredibly real? Some speculative theories suggest that our dreams could be windows into alternate realities, allowing us to briefly experience life in parallel universes.

Supernova

Did you know the gold in your jewelry and the iron in your blood come from exploding stars? Supernovae create the heavy elements that make up much of the material we find on Earth.

For a short time, a single supernova can shine brighter than an entire galaxy with billions of stars! That is how powerful these explosions are.

If a supernova exploded close enough to Earth (within 30 light-years), its radiation could harm our atmosphere. Luckily, there are no stars near us that are likely to explode anytime soon!

In 1987, a supernova exploded in a nearby galaxy, and it was so bright that people could see it without a telescope. It was one of the brightest cosmic events in modern times.
